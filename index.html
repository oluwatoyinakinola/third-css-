DR. NORMAN BORLAUG

The man who saved a billion lives

Dr. Norman Borrlaug,third from the left,trains biologist in Mexico on how to increase wheat yields-part of his life-long war on hunger.

Here is a time line of Dr. borlaug's life:

  • 1914-Born in cresco,Iowa
  • 1933-Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnisota, thanks to a Depression era programme known as the "National Youth Administration"
  • 1935-Has to stop school and save up more money.Works in the Civilian Conservation Corps,helping starving Americans."I saw how food changed them",he said "All of this left scars on me."
  • 1937-Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 1938-Marries wife of 69years Margret Gibson. Gets laid off due to budget cuts. Inspired by Elvin Charles Stackman,he returns to school study under Stackman,who teaches him about breeding pest-resistance plants.
  • 1941-Tries to enrol in the military after the pearl harbor attack,but is rejected. instead,the military asked his lab to work on waterproof glue,DDT to control malaria,disinfectants,and other applied science.
  • 1942-Recieves a PHD. in Genetics and Plant Pathology.
  • 1944-Reject a 100% increase from Dupont, leaves behind his pregnant wife,and flies to mexico to head a new plant Pathology programme,Over the next 16years,his team breeds 6,000 different strains of disease resistant wheat-including different varieties for major climate on Earth.
  • 1945-Discovers a way to grow wheat twice each season, doubling wheat yields.
  • 1953-crosses a short,sturdy dwarf breed of wheat with a high-yielding American breed,creating a strain that responds well to fertilizer,it goes on to provide 95% of Mexico's wheat.
  • 1962-Visit Delhi and brings his high-yielding wheat to the indian subcontinent in time to help mitigate mass starvation due to a rapidly expanding population.
  • 1970-Recieves The Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1983-helps seven African countries dramatically their maize and sorghum yields.
  • 1984-Beccomes a distinguished professor at Texas A&M university
  • 2005-States"we will have to double the world food supply by 2050' Argues that genetically modified crops are the only way we can meet the demand,as we run out of arable land.Says that GM crops are not inherently dangerous because "we,have been genetically modifying plants and animals for a long time before we called it science,people are selecting the best breeds."
  • 2009-dies at the age of 95years.

Borlaug's life and archievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution of one man's towering intellect,persistence and scientific vision can make to human peace and progress."
